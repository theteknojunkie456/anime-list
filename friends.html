How do I get a free key? (2 minutes)

Groq is free, takes about two minutes, and doesn't ask for a credit card.

1
Open console.groq.com in your browser.
2
Tap Sign up — use your Google account, or an email address. Confirm the email if it asks.
3
Once you're in, go to console.groq.com/keys (it's "API Keys" in the menu).
4
Tap Create API Key. Give it any name — "WatchList" is fine.
5
Copy it straight away. It starts with gsk_, and Groq only ever shows it once — if you close the box you'll just make a new one, which is fine.
6
Come back here, paste it in the box above, tap Save. Done.

Prefer somewhere else? aistudio.google.com (key starts AIza) and openrouter.ai (sk-or-) are also free, and paid Claude keys (sk-ant-) work too. The app recognises which is which on its own.

How it works

Everything you watch and read, in one place — with covers, air times and progress filled in for you.

Add anything
Tap + and type a title. Autofill pulls the real cover, episode count, genres and watch order — anime, TV, movies, manga, manhwa and webtoons. No setup, no API key.
Back up your list — do this first
Your list lives on this device only. Clearing your browser data, or deleting the app from your Home Screen, erases it. Turning on cloud backup gives you a sync code: save that code and your list can always be restored, and it follows you to any device.
Watch or Read
The switch beside the search box flips between your watch list and your reading list. They're kept completely separate.
Never miss an episode
The calendar shows what airs this week for the shows you follow, in your own time zone — and it tracks delays. Turn on the bell to get a notification the moment one drops.
Find your next one
The compass suggests titles you don't have yet, picked from what you already watch — real community recommendations, not guesses.
Watch it right here
Open any anime and tap Watch now — it plays inside the app, jumping straight to your next episode. Prefer a different site? Settings → Streaming source lets you swap it for your own.
Make it yours
The palette repaints the whole app — Naruto orange, Sasuke purple, Zoro green and more. Or build your own: pick any accent and background, name it, and it generates the rest. Each title also quietly borrows its own artwork's colour as you browse.
Ask it things (optional)
There's a chat that knows your list — what to watch next, what's short tonight, what fits your mood. It needs a free key (about two minutes, no card); Settings → AI walks you through it step by step. Everything else works without one.
Lock it (optional)
A password encrypts your whole list — unreadable without it, here and in the cloud. You'll get a one-time recovery key; it's the only way back in if you forget the password. Save both. Lose both and the list is gone for good — there's no backdoor. On iPhone you can turn on Face ID so it's one glance to open.
